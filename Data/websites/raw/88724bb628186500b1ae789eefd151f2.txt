                        ©
                    Flickr/ ivanwalsh</a></span></div></div></div></div><div class="l-main m-oh"><div class="l-wrap m-clear"><div class="l-maincolumn m-static"><div class="b-article"><div class="b-article__refs-text"><a class="b-article__refs-rubric" href="/analysis/" itemprop="articleSection">Opinion</a><div class="b-article__refs-credits"><time class="b-article__refs-date" itemprop="dateCreated" datetime="2017-10-14T16:25">16:25 14.10.2017</time><!--noindex--><span data-article="1058234396" id="getShortUrl" class="b-article__refs-getshorturl">Get short URL</span><!--/noindex--></div><div class="b-counters "><a href="/analysis/201710141058234396-china-economy-liberalization-plans/#comments" class="b-counters-icon b-counters-icon_comments"><i></i>2</a><span class="b-counters-icon b-counters-icon_view"><i></i>1381</span><span class="b-counters-icon b-counters-icon_like"><i></i>14</span><span class="b-counters-icon b-counters-icon_dislike"><i></i>0</span></div></div><div itemprop="description" class="b-article__lead"><p>China is considering more economic liberalization reforms and discussions on the issue are currently underway in the national leadership. According to experts, a more open economy is the key to keeping China’s growth steady amid  new global and domestic challenges.</p></div><div itemprop="articleBody" class="b-article__text"><p>Zhou Xiaochuan, governor of&nbsp;the People&rsquo;s Bank of&nbsp;China, said in&nbsp;a recent interview with&nbsp;the Chinese influential economic magazine Caijing that China should implement three reforms, including trade liberalization, investment liberalization and yuan liberalization.</p>
<p>At the same time, foreign governments <a href="http://sputniknews.com/politics/20170718/1055662974.html" target="_blank">often criticize China</a> for&nbsp;slowing down&nbsp;the process of&nbsp;opening its economy to&nbsp;the global market. According to&nbsp;Alexander Lomanov, an expert at&nbsp;the Institute for&nbsp;Far Eastern Studies, the Russian Academy of&nbsp;Sciences, Zhou&rsquo;s remarks came as&nbsp;a signal to&nbsp;foreign governments and indicates that Beijing is likely to&nbsp;deal with&nbsp;the issue.</p>
<p class="marker-quote1">"His message was that new measures on&nbsp;developing a market economy and liberalizing trade will be discussed at&nbsp;the upcoming party congress. For the Chinese leadership, the priority goal is to&nbsp;adjust its economic reform due to&nbsp;new global and domestic economic challenges," Lomanov told <a rel="nofollow" href="http://sputniknews.cn/" target="_blank">Sputnik China</a>.</p>
<p><div class="b-inject m-inject-min"><div class="b-inject__media"><img src="https://cdn4.img.sputniknews.com/images/105360/36/1053603630.jpg" width="705" height="375" alt="Chinese President Xi Jinping makes a toast at the beginning of the welcoming banquet at the Great Hall of the People during the first day of the Belt and Road Forum in Beijing, China, May 14, 2017" title="Chinese President Xi Jinping makes a toast at the beginning of the welcoming banquet at the Great Hall of the People during the first day of the Belt and Road Forum in Beijing, China, May 14, 2017" class=""></div><div class="b-inject__copy"><span>
                        ©
                    REUTERS/ Damir Sagolj </span></div><div class="b-inject__content"><a href="/politics/201709011057006669-china-brics-summit-india-xiamen/">How China Translating Economic Development Into Geopolitical Gains</a></div></div>However, what is more important, according to&nbsp;the expert, is the fact that the importance of&nbsp;new economic reforms is not questioned by&nbsp;the Chinese leadership.</p>
<p class="marker-quote1">"This is not a polemic matter. First of&nbsp;all, China wants to&nbsp;continue its current policy and wants to&nbsp;solidify its unprecedented experience of&nbsp;economic growth of&nbsp;40 years. The driving factor behind&nbsp;this policy is the need to&nbsp;maintain growth stability and sustainable economic development," he pointed out.</p>
<p>According to&nbsp;data from&nbsp;China&rsquo;s central bank, Chinese foreign exchange reserves reached over $3.1 trillion and have been increasing for&nbsp;the seventh straight month. The South China Morning Post reported citing experts that taking into&nbsp;account this tendency, Beijing may soon ease some of&nbsp;its regulations, including on&nbsp;payments and investments.</p>
<p><div class="b-inject m-inject-min"><div class="b-inject__media"><img src="https://cdn4.img.sputniknews.com/images/105243/44/1052434420.jpg" width="705" height="375" alt="U.S. President Donald Trump welcomes Chinese President Xi Jinping at Mar-a-Lago state in Palm Beach, Florida, U.S., April 6, 2017." title="U.S. President Donald Trump welcomes Chinese President Xi Jinping at Mar-a-Lago state in Palm Beach, Florida, U.S., April 6, 2017." class=""></div><div class="b-inject__copy"><span>
                        ©
                    REUTERS/ Carlos Barria</span></div><div class="b-inject__content"><a href="/business/201707191055673445-us-china-economic-talks/">'Global Growth in Big Trouble' if US, China Fail to Coordinate Economic Policies</a></div></div>The increase in&nbsp;China&rsquo;s forex reserves is the result of&nbsp;its stable economic growth and measures against&nbsp;capital outflow, according to&nbsp;Bian Yongzu, an expert with&nbsp;the Renmin University of&nbsp;China.</p>
<p class="marker-quote1">"An increase in&nbsp;forex reserves is a sign of&nbsp;a stable economy and positive trends in&nbsp;global trade. Moreover, the yuan strengthened in&nbsp;the first half of&nbsp;2017. All of&nbsp;the above&nbsp;means that the <a href="http://sputniknews.com/business/20170627/1055007585.html" target="_blank">Chinese economy</a> has a large potential for&nbsp;growth. It also indicates that the Chinese government has the political will to&nbsp;maintain stable economic growth. This is an important signal for&nbsp;the rest of&nbsp;the world," Bian said.</p>
<p>Earlier this week, Ning Jizhe, head of&nbsp;the Chinese Statistics Bureau, said that Beijing will have no problem meeting its economic growth target of&nbsp;6.5 percent this year and may even exceed it. Last week, the World Bank improved its outlook for&nbsp;China&rsquo;s growth for&nbsp;2017, from&nbsp;6.5 to&nbsp;6.7 percent.</p></div><div class="b-banner m-banner-33 m-mb20 m-mt10"><!--В head сайта один раз подключите библиотеку-->
